1. VNet Address Space: For example, 10.0.0.0/16 gives 65,536 IPs, ranging from 10.0.0.0 to 10.0.255.255.

2. VM Subnet: If 100 IPs are required, we use a /25 subnet (128 IPs, 123 usable after Azure reserves 5).

3. Bastion Subnet: Needs a minimum of /27 (32 IPs), so we assign 10.0.0.128/27.

4. Firewall Subnet: Requires at least /26 (64 IPs), so we assign 10.0.0.160/26.

5. GatewaySubnet: Typically a /27 for basic VPN setups, assigned to 10.0.0.224/27.

Note: Remaining space from the /16 block is still available for future use. Subnets are aligned with Azure best practices.
